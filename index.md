## About

I am currently a Senior Quantitative User Experience Researcher on the Search Ads team at Google. In practice, I act as a mixture of applied cognitive scientist, computational linguist, machine learning engineer, and quant research methodologist for work that I am not allowed to share. ;)

By training, I am a computational cognitive neuroscientist with an auditory focus (PhD at McMaster with Laurel Trainor; postdoc at NYU with David Poeppel). Fundamentally, my research asks how the brain decodes the acoustic world: how we process speech and music, and how we use them to interact. The single, continuous theme tying my work together is that time is the core infrastructure of these phenomena, from prediction to production. I've dragged this focus into the algorithmic realm, digging into computational models of audition and their representations in both brains and machines. I am a methodological pluralist: jamming on behavioral experiments, neuroimaging, signal processing, ML/AI, and computational modeling as needed, and I prefer simplicity. My work has been recognized by the National Institutes of Health, the New York Academy of Sciences, the Governor General of Canada, the Natural Sciences and Engineering Research Council of Canada, the GRAMMY Foundation, and the Cognitive Neuroscience Society, and has been featured in Scientific American.

Beyond, I am a humbly okay classical violinist/violist, an over-enthusiastic jazz learner, a terrible squash player, an improving-maybe husband, and a definetly goofy daddy of one (soon to be two).

## Research Interests

- **sound & brain** — the acoustic, perceptual, and neural features that tell speech and music apart, and what that (dis)similarity means for people with communication disorders. Also how the brain tracks rhythm, pitch, and temporal structure to predict what comes next.
- **interpersonal interaction** — how dynamic vocal, facial, and body behavior reveal leadership, emotion, and connection during multipersonal joint music performance, conversation, videoconferencing, and dating <3.
- **ml/ai** — interpretable machine learning and its representations with an audio focus, ideally with neuroscientific and cognitive inspirations.
- **research methods** — exploring ad libitum to find the mathematically sound and just-slightly-more-than-enough approach to serve the research need.
